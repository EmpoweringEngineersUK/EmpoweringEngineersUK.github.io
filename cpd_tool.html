Strategic CPD for UK-SPEC Registration

Continuing Professional Development (CPD) is a mandatory requirement for maintaining CEng, IEng, and EngTech status with the Engineering Council. However, many applicants fail to demonstrate a balanced portfolio. This tool utilizes the 70/20/10 Learning Model to ensure your development is robust and practical.

The 70/20/10 Methodology

  • 70% Experiential (On-the-Job): The majority of your engineering competence comes from solving real-world problems, leading projects, and fixing site issues. This is where you prove "application" of knowledge.
  • 20% Social (Peer Learning): Learning from others through mentoring, attending design reviews, or shadowing senior engineers. This demonstrates your ability to work within a professional hierarchy.
  • 10% Formal (Training): Classroom learning, webinars, and academic courses. While essential for theoretical foundations, formal training alone is insufficient for Chartership.

Reflection is Key

Simply logging hours is not enough. For every activity generated by this planner, you must record a Reflective Statement: What did you learn? How have you applied it? How has it benefited your organization or the wider public? This "Cycle of Reflection" is what professional reviewers are looking for during your interview.
